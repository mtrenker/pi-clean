# Context playbook

Use this reference to choose a direction, not to select a preset. Product types can combine; identify which moment currently owns the interface.

## Experience centers

| Center | Design around | Invite through | Typical emotional risk | Where expression can live |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Task and workspace | throughput, orientation, command, undo, continuity | a clear next action and visible current state | overload, loss of control, interruption | interaction details, spatial organization, domain-specific tools |
| Conversation and community | people, reciprocity, safety, social context | previews, familiar participation, low-risk first contribution | exposure, ambiguity, hostility, notification pressure | identity, voice, reactions, community artifacts |
| Reading and knowledge | comprehension, sequence, provenance, return paths | a strong premise, useful preview, comfortable entry | fatigue, disorientation, dubious authority | typography, pacing, illustration, editorial composition |
| Commerce and service | evaluation, confidence, commitment, fulfillment | transparent value, comparison, availability, low-commitment exploration | manipulation, hidden cost, regret, payment anxiety | product imagery, tactile detail, curation, post-purchase care |
| Learning and progress | understanding, practice, feedback, agency | a reachable first challenge and visible purpose | shame, false progress, reward fatigue | examples, milestones, constructive response, subject metaphor |
| Care and sensitive administration | comprehension, dignity, privacy, informed consent | plain expectations, human reassurance, visible help | fear, stigma, irreversible mistakes, abandonment | calm language, continuity, supportive illustration used sparingly |
| Monitoring and analysis | anomaly, trend, comparison, drill-down, action | an intelligible overview with clear paths to evidence | alarm fatigue, false certainty, data density | data encoding, annotations, domain-specific spatial models |
| Event, culture, and exhibit | anticipation, atmosphere, discovery, memory | a sensory cue plus immediate orientation | spectacle obscuring access or logistics | composition, imagery, sound/motion with consent, transitions |
| Creative and immersive | exploration, making, play, surprise, authorship | a compelling affordance and safe first experiment | novelty tax, getting lost, inaccessible interaction | canvas behavior, transformation, spatial or narrative motifs |
| Marketing and portfolio | relevance, credibility, story, commitment | a specific promise with evidence and an obvious path | generic claims, inflated spectacle, premature conversion | narrative rhythm, art direction, content-derived demonstrations, proof |

Do not force an entire product into one center. A commerce checkout becomes a high-trust transaction; a learning analytics view becomes monitoring; a community report flow becomes sensitive administration. Shift the posture with the moment while retaining the same design language.

## Translate emotion into evidence

Emotional words are hypotheses until the interface demonstrates them.

| Desired feeling | Interaction evidence | Sensory/visual support | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Invited | low-risk entry, clear expectations, useful preview, optional depth | open composition, legible entry point, human-scale language | vague welcome screens, premature signup, empty cheerfulness |
| Reassured | visible status, reversibility, privacy cues, honest timelines, recovery | stable rhythm, calm contrast, restrained motion | pastel-as-empathy, false certainty, hiding consequences |
| Capable | direct manipulation, sensible defaults, shortcuts, clear feedback | strong hierarchy, precise alignment, compact controls where earned | infantilizing copy, excessive hand-holding, ornamental friction |
| Curious | meaningful reveal, progressive depth, clues, safe exploration | contrast in scale, editorial pacing, suggestive imagery | mystery around basic controls, novelty everywhere |
| Energized | visible momentum, responsive feedback, attainable next step | decisive color, rhythm, directional composition | constant animation, urgent color on everything, reward spam |
| Focused | protected primary task, low interruption, remembered context | quiet peripheral UI, controlled measure, reduced competing accents | feature walls, decorative dashboards, hidden navigation |
| Delighted | timely recognition, transformation, wit, satisfying completion | one surprising but coherent detail, expressive transition | confetti by default, jokes in failure states, repeated novelty |
| Cared for | respectful choices, patient errors, escape/help, continuity | warm but dignified language, gentle pacing, clear separation | forced positivity, cuteness during distress, manipulative reassurance |
| Trusting | provenance, transparent limits/cost, confirmation, auditability | consistency, readable details, sober semantic states | badges without evidence, dark patterns, aesthetic authority alone |

A single journey may need an arc: curious while exploring, capable while acting, reassured while committing, and quietly satisfied after completion. Do not apply one mood uniformly.

## Construct a distinctive grammar

Choose two or three moves with a reason, then repeat them selectively.

- **Composition:** asymmetry, strict grid, layered sequence, continuous canvas, dense rail, generous reading column.
- **Typography:** role contrast, editorial scale, utilitarian labels, numeric emphasis, conversational cadence.
- **Color/material:** ink-on-paper, luminous field, tactile border, flat diagram, muted workspace with vivid action, image-led surface.
- **Behavior:** progressive reveal, direct manipulation, persistent context, staged ceremony, playful transformation, rapid command response.
- **Content/art direction:** domain diagrams, documentary photography, user artifacts, annotated examples, illustration with a consistent hand.

Apply an artifact test: can the central claim become visible as a diagram, specimen, annotated example, before/after relationship, or interaction? A useful artifact compresses understanding or provides evidence. If it only repeats the headline decoratively, remove it.

Use a removal test: if the logo and product name disappeared, would the interface still express something specific about this product? If not, deepen the relationship to its content or behavior rather than adding more effects.

## Direction checks

Before implementation, ask:

- Which center owns this moment, and what changes in adjacent moments?
- What should be immediate, and what should unfold?
- Where is attention welcome, and where would it be disrespectful?
- Which familiar convention lowers risk? Where is novelty worth its learning cost?
- What is the one detail a person could remember and accurately associate with this product?
- Can each signature move be expressed across states and viewports, not only in a presentation screenshot?
- Which choices are genuinely required by context, and which are user-owned preferences best decided from rendered alternatives?
